Principles, Personal

Principles

The following principles guide my work:
  • “Health is a human right. The public health community exists to safeguard that right.” Public health research must aim to create a more just and equitable world.
  • Sharing reproduction achives and statistical code & producing publicly available datasets (when appropriate) to create a more rigorous and inclusive scientific environment.
  • Supporting and advocating for data soverignty for indigenous people and nations.
  • Supporting and mentoring the next generation of scientists and public health professionals, from high school through graduate school, to create a more just, equitable, and inclusive scientific environment.
  • Naming and describing how institutional, structural, and interpersonal racism, classism, misogyny, xenophobia, & transphobia operate & intersect to impact health and cause disease.
